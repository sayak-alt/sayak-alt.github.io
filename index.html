About

I am an experimental high-energy nuclear physicist with experience in detector R&D, data acquisition, electronics integration, and Monte Carlo simulation. My work includes Gas Electron Multiplier (GEM), RPC, Cherenkov, straw-tube, and scintillator-based detector systems, along with GEANT4-based simulation, calibration, signal processing, and data analysis for precision measurements.

I am particularly interested in building and characterizing instrumentation for high-rate environments, connecting detector performance to physics reach, and contributing to next-generation experimental programs.

Research Interests

  • Detector R&D: GEM, RPC, Cherenkov, straw-tube, and scintillator systems
  • Monte Carlo simulation, reconstruction, and performance optimization
  • DAQ systems, readout electronics, calibration, and signal processing
  • Precision electroweak measurements and nucleon/nuclear structure studies

Current Research

My current work focuses on precision electroweak measurements in the MOLLER experiment at Jefferson Lab, including Cherenkov detector characterization, photon-yield studies, cosmic-ray test stand development, and DAQ analysis workflows. I am also interested in future studies at the Electron-Ion Collider, especially near-threshold J/ψ production, detector feasibility studies, and structure-function measurements.
